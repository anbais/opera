Most Important Operas in the Kazakh History

Abay, Kyz Zhibek, and Birzhan-Sara are among the best achievements of the opera art of Kazakhstan.

Abay

❮ ❯

Opera by Akhmet Zhubanov and Latif Hamidi

Libretto by Mukhtar Auezov

The opera "Abay" is a story of a doomed romance of two young people - Aydar and Azhar - in the context of the eternal struggle between conservatism and reformism. The beautiful scores allow one to experience a wide array of emotions - love, anxiety, joy, hope, and bitterness of loss.

The opera premiered in 1944 on the stage of the Kazakh Academic Opera and Ballet Theater in Almaty.

Kyz Zhibek

❮ ❯

Opera by Yevgeny Brusilovsky

Libretto by Gabit Musrepov

"Kyz Zhibek" is based on a legend taking place in the beginning of the XVI century when the Kazakh Khanate was formed from many steppe clans and tribes. It tells a story about a brave warrior, Tolegen, and a young beauty, Zhibek, whose romantic relationships end tragically due to an inter-family stife.

The opera premiered in 1934 on the stage of the Kazakh Academic Opera and Ballet Theater in Almaty.

Birzhan-Sara

❮ ❯

Opera by Mukan Tulebayev

Libretto by Khazhim Zhumaliev

Inspired by a true story, "Birzhan-Sara" centers around a XIX-century poetical contest (aitys) between two famous singer-songwriters, Birzhan and Sara. Utilizing many traditional musical styles, the opera explores several social and moral issues.

The opera premiered in 1946 on the stage of the Kazakh Academic Opera and Ballet Theater in Almaty.
